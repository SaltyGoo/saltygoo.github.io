##### I am like 2 years late but who cares!

I really love GLOG classes, and the community in the [GLOG Discord](https://discord.gg/HKNQ8GG6bW) is so creative, it needs to be archived somewhere! Here's a compilation of all the 142 classes that have been published on it in 2023! The **Types**—Specialist (**S**), Wizard (**W**) and Fighter (**F**)—have been arbitrarily given by me after reading each class. 

Like last year, I noted 10 who particularly inspired me and that I will probably steal from:

- Blatella's **[SPECTER](https://mascaraknight.blogspot.com/2023/09/this-is-who-i-really-am-glitterglog.html)**. A mall goth's edgy fantasy. This class is so funny as a concept, but also gameplay-wise. Every ability is so flavourful and makes me want to play it.
- Deus Ex Parabola's **[DEMIGOD](https://as-they-must.blogspot.com/2023/06/on-great-men-and-little-gods-glog-class.html)**. I think it's pretty challenging to make a demigod class seem fair, and this really achieves that. Plus it is modular in a way that feeds into the world's mythology.
- J.N.Sinombre's **[WARLOCK](https://madgods.bearblog.dev/glog-class-warlock/)**. To be honest, J.N. made many great classes this year (shoutout to the Revolutionary and the Rabid druid), but I just love warlocks. This warlock design really understand the idea of making a pact for immediate power and living with constant fear of payback.
- Keyorden's **[SCAPEGOAT](https://keyordenthefox.blogspot.com/2023/04/glog-class-scape-goat.html)**. This seems to be the only post on their blog, but I am fascinated with the design of a class that relies on failing. It would totally change the way you interact with the game, and that is the sign of a good class.
- Lexi's **[SAPPER](https://crateredland.blogspot.com/2023/05/the-sapper.html)**. As someone who loves drawing dungeon maps, this class is for me. It also makes me excited to see how a sapper could break the game by changing the environment.
- Loch's **[ALIENIST](https://nothicseye.blogspot.com/2023/08/inadvisable-decisions-glg.html)**. Another one where it was hard to choose (shoutout to the Ploutocrat and the Mystic). The Delta class trend where each class ability requires accomplishing something is perfect flavour for a scientist's maddening research.
- Mavell Duceau's **[BABY YAGA](https://oneeyetotheanchor.blogspot.com/2023/04/glog-class-baby-yaga.html)**. Be a witch's hut! So fun, and perfect for a player duo.
- Morgan's **[MONSTER BLOOD](https://dungeonfruit.blogspot.com/2023/08/blood-of-fang.html)**. It uses the Delta class format to allow players to gain powers from monsters they defeat. It makes me want to design more templates for each dungeon my players go through.
- The First Gokun's **[URBANIST](https://spiceomancy.blogspot.com/2023/06/ruined-city-navigating-glog-class.html)** I think exploring the design space outside of combat abilities is pretty cool, and this class reaaly encourages interacting with the world. In this case, the architectural styles of a mega city.
- Vivanter's **[COUNSELOR](https://mediumsandmessages.bearblog.dev/glog-the-counselor/)**. Another non-violent class, and a very elegant one. I always feel like there's a curious, more quiet player at my table who would love that, and as a DM, I just WANT them to ask me the questions about my world.
